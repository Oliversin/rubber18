Rubber Extrusion

Rubber extrusion is a manufacturing process in which raw rubber material is forced through a mold (or die) to create continuous shapes with a specific profile. This technique is commonly used to produce rubber seals, gaskets, hoses, tubing, and custom profiles for various industries, including automotive, construction, and medical devices. The extrusion process ensures high precision and consistency, allowing for the creation of complex cross-sectional shapes in various sizes and lengths.

Precision Extrusion Services: Printing, Cutting, and Splicing Solutions

We have ink jet printers for applying logos or other prints that need to be directly applied to an extrusion. We have automated equipment for cutting extrusions to length. We can package your extrusions in bulk, in individual bags with other components, or however is the most convenient for your company. We also have equipment for splicing ends of an extrusion together. This is often done for large diameter seals where molds the extrusion would be cost prohibitive. We have equipment, techniques and adhesives to ensure that the spliced ends are done accurately and will not fail. We are able to splice ends with silicone as well as with organic rubbers including EPDM, and NBR.
